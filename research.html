Research

My career has allowed me to focus on a variety of research interests, most of them stemming from my work as a research and instruction librarian. These concepts are presented here with more information about the deliverables I have created as part of each overarching topic.

Information Behaviors of SGM Individuals in Substance Use Recovery Programs

In my doctoral research, I explored the sense-making behaviors of sexual and gender minority (SGM) individuals pursuing remission from substance use disorder, with a particular interest in the impact of minority stress on these behaviors. To conduct my research, I applied a transformative framework to collecting and analyzing oral histories within an SGM-affirming twelve-step program community.

"A Great Place to Meet": Oral Histories of Sexual and Gender Minority Members of a Twelve-Step Program Clubhouse, Dissertation
"Stigma & Health Information Seeking Behaviors of LGBTQ+ Individuals with Substance Use Concerns," Presentation to the Doctoral Student Association at Simmons University School of Library and Information Science. Also my first experiment with markdown for presentation slides.
"Queering Health Information Seeking," Independent study with Dr. Colin Rhinesmith about applying queer theory to the study of health information-seeking behaviors. Contains my syllabus for the course.
